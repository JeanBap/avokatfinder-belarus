Zemelny Uchastok (Зямельны ўчастак)

A land plot or parcel of land in Belarus

Zemelny uchastok (land plot) refers to a defined area of land that can be owned, leased, or used under various legal titles in Belarus. Land law in Belarus is complex because much land is state-owned; private ownership of land is permitted but subject to significant restrictions depending on the land's category and location.

Belarusian law permits private ownership of land plots designated for individual residential construction (IZhS — individualnoe zhilishchnoe stroitelstvo), personal subsidiary farming, horticulture, and certain commercial purposes. Agricultural land is generally not privately ownable by individuals.

How It Works in Practice

Land plots in Belarus are allocated for private ownership or long-term lease through auction processes administered by local executive committees. Auctions are the standard mechanism for obtaining a new land plot for construction. The winning bidder receives a land allocation decision (reshenie o predostavlenii zemelnogo uchastka).

The cadastral value of a land plot (kadastrovaya stoimost) is determined by the State Property Committee based on land category, location, and permitted use. The cadastral value is used as the basis for land tax calculations and for transfer tax on land sales.

Land Title DocumentGosudarstvennyy akt na zemlyu or svidetelstvo — verify separately

Buyers of properties that include a land plot must ensure that both the building and the land are properly registered and that the land title document (gosudarstvennyy akt na zemlyu or svidetelstvo) is included in the due diligence package. Buildings and land are often sold together in Belarus, and the land title must be separately verified.

Common Mistake: Buyers of houses sometimes focus only on the building registration and forget to check the land title. In Belarus, the building and land are registered separately, and a missing or defective land title can prevent lawful use or future sale of the property.
Expert Tip: When purchasing a house with land, request both the building registration certificate and the land title document (akt or svidetelstvo) from the seller. Verify that the land boundaries in the cadastral plan match the physical boundaries visible on site.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a foreigner privately own land in Belarus?

Foreigners face significant restrictions on land ownership in Belarus. Foreign citizens generally cannot own agricultural land and face limitations on residential land ownership. Long-term lease is more commonly available to foreigners.

How is a land plot allocated for construction in Belarus?

Land is allocated through an auction process administered by the local executive committee. The winning bidder receives a land allocation decision and subsequently a state certificate of land use or ownership.

What is the gosudarstvennyy akt na zemlyu?

It is the state certificate of land ownership or use rights, the primary document confirming a private individual's legal relationship with a specific land plot in Belarus.
