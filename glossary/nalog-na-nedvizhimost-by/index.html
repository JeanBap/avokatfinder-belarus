Nalog na Nedvizhimost (Падатак на нерухомасць)

Annual real estate tax on property ownership in Belarus

Nalog na nedvizhimost is the annual property tax levied on owners of real estate in Belarus. Both individuals and legal entities owning real property are subject to this tax, though the rates, calculation methods, and filing requirements differ significantly between the two categories.

For individual owners, the tax is calculated by the local tax authority based on the property's assessed value (as determined by the State Property Committee) and the applicable rate. Individuals receive a tax notice and pay annually. The tax must be paid by 15 November of the tax year.

How It Works in Practice

Standard rates for individuals vary by property type and local government decisions. Residential property rates are typically modest — often a fraction of one percent of assessed value per year — making the annual tax burden for most homeowners relatively low. Local authorities may apply coefficients that adjust the standard rate up or down.

Certain categories of owners qualify for exemptions or reductions, including pensioners, disabled persons, large families, and veterans. Buyers should confirm eligibility for any exemption with the local tax authority and ensure the exemption is applied to their account after purchase.

Key Facts

Annual Deadline15 November of the tax year for individuals
RateFraction of assessed value per year; varies by type and locality
ExemptionsPensioners, disabled, large families, veterans — apply to local tax authority
Legal Entities1% of accounting value; quarterly advance payments
AssessmentState Property Committee determines assessed value

Legal entities owning real property face a different regime, with quarterly advance payments and a rate of 1% of the property's accounting value. Commercial property buyers should factor ongoing property tax into their investment return calculations.

Common Mistake: New owners sometimes forget to notify the tax authority of their purchase, resulting in tax bills continuing to arrive in the previous owner's name. Notify the local tax authority of the ownership change immediately after state registration.
Expert Tip: Apply for any applicable tax exemption (nalogovy vychet or lgota) promptly after purchase. Exemptions are not applied automatically — you must submit an application to the local tax authority with proof of eligibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays nalog na nedvizhimost in Belarus?

All owners of real property — both individuals and legal entities — are subject to the annual property tax. Rates and filing rules differ between individual and corporate owners.

When must individuals pay the property tax?

Individual property owners must pay by 15 November of the tax year, based on a tax notice issued by the local tax authority.

Are there exemptions from property tax in Belarus?

Yes. Pensioners, disabled persons, large families, and veterans may qualify for exemptions or reduced rates. Applications must be submitted to the local tax authority.
