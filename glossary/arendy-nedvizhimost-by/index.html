Arenda Nedvizhimosti (Арэнда нерухомасці)

A real estate lease or rental agreement in Belarus

Arenda nedvizhimosti refers to lease or rental agreements for real property in Belarus. The tenant (arendator) pays rent (arendnaya plata) to the landlord (arendodatel) for the right to use the property for a defined period. Lease agreements are governed by Belarus's Civil Code and, for specific types of property, by separate legislation.

Commercial leases for premises used in business activity are subject to specific regulation in Belarus, including pricing rules that have historically restricted landlords' ability to freely set commercial rents. Government-owned commercial property is leased at rates set by local authorities according to established formulae.

How It Works in Practice

Residential leases are governed by the Housing Code of Belarus, which provides minimum protections for tenants including restrictions on eviction, notice periods, and the right to renew. Landlords cannot increase rent during a fixed-term lease unless the agreement expressly permits it.

Leases of state or municipal land for commercial or construction purposes (arendnaya plata za zemlyu) are a distinct category requiring separate agreements with the relevant state authority. Land lease fees are calculated according to government tariffs and are reviewed periodically.

Long-term leases (over one year) for any real property must be registered in the unified state register. Unregistered long-term leases are not enforceable against third parties, including a new owner who purchases the property during the lease term.

Common Mistake: Landlords of commercial property sometimes set rents in foreign currency equivalents to circumvent Belarusian ruble depreciation. Tenants should clarify exactly how rent is calculated and what exchange rate applies to avoid disputes.
Expert Tip: For residential tenancy, request a written lease even for short-term agreements. A verbal agreement offers very limited legal protection in a dispute over deposit return or early termination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are long-term leases registered in Belarus?

Yes. Leases for more than one year must be registered in the unified state register. Without registration, the lease is not enforceable against third parties or a new property owner.

Can a landlord increase rent during a fixed-term residential lease?

Not unless the lease agreement explicitly permits it. The Housing Code restricts rent increases during a fixed term.

What governs commercial leases in Belarus?

The Civil Code provides the general framework. Government restrictions on commercial rent levels — particularly for state-owned property — add regulatory complexity on top of the contractual terms.
