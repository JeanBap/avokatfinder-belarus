Podohodny Nalog s Prodazhi (Падаходны падатак з продажу)

Income tax on real estate sale proceeds in Belarus

When a property is sold in Belarus, the seller may be liable to pay income tax (podohodny nalog) on the gain from the sale. The tax applies to the proceeds if the seller has not owned the property for the required minimum holding period or if it is not their primary residence.

Belarusian tax law provides that individuals who sell residential real estate that they have owned and used as their primary residence for at least three years are generally exempt from income tax on the sale proceeds. Sales of secondary properties or investment properties within the minimum holding period are taxable.

How It Works in Practice

The applicable income tax rate for property sale gains by individuals in Belarus is 13%. The taxable base is the difference between the sale price and the documented acquisition cost (adjusted for inflation in some cases). Sellers who cannot document their acquisition cost face tax assessed on the full sale price.

Non-residents of Belarus selling Belarusian property are subject to tax on the sale gain at the non-resident rate. Belarus has double tax treaties with many countries that may reduce the withholding rate — sellers who are tax residents of a treaty country should verify their entitlement before completing the sale.

Key Facts

ExemptionPrimary residence owned 3+ years — generally exempt
Rate13% on gain for taxable sales by individuals
Tax BaseSale price minus documented acquisition cost
Non-ResidentsSubject to tax; double tax treaties may apply
FilingSeller files tax return; tax does not encumber the property post-sale

The seller is responsible for filing a tax return and paying the tax. Buyers are not typically responsible for the seller's income tax but should be aware that an unpaid tax liability of the seller does not attach to the property after sale — unlike transfer taxes, which are checked before registration.

Common Mistake: Sellers sometimes forget to check whether their sale is exempt before agreeing a price net of tax. A sale within the minimum holding period triggers a 13% tax, which can significantly affect net proceeds. Calculate this before pricing the property.
Expert Tip: Keep all original purchase documentation — contract, registration certificate, receipts for improvements — to document your acquisition cost and support an exemption claim. Missing documentation can result in tax being assessed on the full sale price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is residential property sale in Belarus tax-free?

It can be — if the seller has owned and used the property as their primary residence for at least three years. Other sales may be subject to 13% income tax on the gain.

What rate of income tax applies to a taxable property sale?

The rate is 13% of the net gain (sale price minus documented acquisition cost) for individual sellers.

Does unpaid seller income tax attach to the property?

No. Unlike transfer taxes, income tax is a personal liability of the seller and does not encumber the property after the sale is completed and registered.
