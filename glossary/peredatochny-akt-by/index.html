Peredatochny Akt (Перадатачны акт)

The transfer deed documenting handover of real estate possession in Belarus

Peredatochny akt (transfer deed or handover act) is a document signed by buyer and seller at the moment of physical possession transfer, confirming that the property has been delivered in the agreed condition. While not always mandatory by statute, it is standard practice in Belarusian real estate transactions and strongly recommended.

The transfer deed records the condition of the property at handover — the state of fixtures, fittings, meters, keys, and any included items of personal property. It also confirms that the seller has vacated the property and that the utility meter readings have been noted for the purposes of settling outstanding bills.

How It Works in Practice

In Belarus, the risk of accidental damage or destruction of the property passes from seller to buyer at the moment of possession transfer. The peredatochny akt establishes this moment precisely, which is important if the property is damaged between signing and handover.

Where a property is sold with existing furniture or appliances, an inventory (opis imushchestva) attached to the transfer deed lists all included items. If any items are missing at handover, the buyer can use the deed to document the deficiency and claim compensation.

The transfer deed is signed in the presence of both parties (or their authorised representatives) and is retained by both buyer and seller. It complements the purchase contract and should be stored with the registration certificate as part of the ownership documentation.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes accept possession without signing a formal transfer deed, trusting the seller's verbal assurances about the property's condition. If defects emerge after handover, the absence of a deed makes it very difficult to establish what was disclosed.
Expert Tip: Inspect the property thoroughly before signing the transfer deed. Note any defects or missing items in the deed itself — a signed record of defects is far more useful than a verbal complaint after the fact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the peredatochny akt legally required in Belarus?

It is not always mandated by statute, but is standard practice and strongly recommended. It establishes the moment of possession transfer and documents the property's condition.

What happens if the property is damaged after signing but before handover?

The seller bears the risk of accidental damage until the peredatochny akt is signed. After signing, the risk passes to the buyer.

Should I note defects in the transfer deed?

Yes. Any defects observed at handover should be explicitly noted in the deed. This creates a record that can support a compensation claim if the seller has not disclosed the defect.
