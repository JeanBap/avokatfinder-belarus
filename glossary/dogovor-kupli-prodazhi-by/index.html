Dogovor Kupli-Prodazhi (Договор купли-продажи)

The purchase and sale contract for real estate in Belarus

Dogovor kupli-prodazhi is the core legal instrument for transferring real estate ownership in Belarus. The contract must be concluded in written form and notarised (or executed at the state registration office) before it can be submitted for state registration of the ownership right.

The contract must identify both parties, describe the property precisely using its cadastral reference, state the agreed price, and set out payment terms and the date of possession transfer. Any encumbrances — mortgages, easements, or court orders — must be disclosed in the contract.

How It Works in Practice

After signing and notarisation, the buyer must submit the contract to the relevant territorial organisation of the State Property Committee (Goskomimushchestvo) for state registration of ownership. Registration is constitutive — the buyer does not legally own the property until registration is complete.

Belarus requires that the purchase price be stated in Belarusian rubles (BYN), even where the parties privately agree a price in another currency. Currency controls and official exchange rate rules affect how the price is calculated and paid in practice.

Key Facts

FormWritten + notarised or executed at state registration office
CurrencyPrice stated in Belarusian rubles (BYN)
Registration BodyState Property Committee (Goskomimushchestvo)
Legal EffectOwnership transfers on state registration, not on signing
Dispute ForumCourts of general jurisdiction or economic courts

Disputes over purchase contracts are resolved by the courts of general jurisdiction or, for commercial property, by economic courts. The notary is obliged to retain the original certified contract in their archive and can be asked to issue additional certified copies.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes assume ownership is complete once the contract is signed and the price is paid. Without completed state registration, the buyer's title is unprotected and the seller's creditors can still claim against the property.
Expert Tip: Request confirmation of state registration completion (svidetelstvo o gosudarstvennoy registratsii) as soon as processing is finished. This document is your proof of legal ownership and should be stored securely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a purchase contract need to be notarised in Belarus?

Yes. The contract must be notarised or executed at the state registration authority before it can form the basis for state registration of ownership.

In what currency must the price be stated?

The price must be stated in Belarusian rubles (BYN). Parties may privately reference a foreign currency equivalent, but the official contract price is in BYN.

When does ownership legally transfer in Belarus?

Ownership transfers on completion of state registration, not when the contract is signed or the price is paid.
