Kadastr Nedvizhimosti (Кадастр недвижимости)

The real estate cadastre recording physical property data in Belarus

Kadastr nedvizhimosti is Belarus's official register of real property, maintained by the State Property Committee and its territorial agencies. It records the physical and technical characteristics of every parcel and building: location, area, boundaries, land category, and building specifications.

The cadastre operates alongside the state registration system for property rights. While the cadastre records physical facts, the state registration (gosudarstvennaya registratsiya) records legal rights. Both must be checked during due diligence, as discrepancies can indicate boundary disputes or unregistered alterations.

How It Works in Practice

The Unified State Register of Immovable Property, Rights Thereto and Transactions Therewith (Yediny Gosudarstvennyy Registr) integrates cadastral and rights data in Belarus, making it easier to obtain a comprehensive extract covering both physical and legal information in one request.

Buyers can request a cadastral extract (vypiska iz kadastrovogo pasporta) from the territorial state registration organisation. The extract confirms the parcel's cadastral number, area, category, and registered owner, and forms a key part of the due diligence documentation package.

For properties with buildings, a separate technical passport (tekhnicheskiy pasport) prepared by a licensed organisation records the building's construction details, floor plans, and area. Buyers should verify that the technical passport reflects the current state of the building, including any extensions or renovations.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes skip the technical passport review, only to discover after purchase that the building's actual layout differs from the registered plans due to unpermitted alterations. Always compare the passport with the physical property.
Expert Tip: Request the technical passport and cadastral extract together. If the property has been renovated or extended without an updated passport, require the seller to regularise this before signing the purchase contract.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Yediny Gosudarstvennyy Registr?

It is Belarus's unified state register that integrates cadastral and property rights data, allowing buyers to obtain comprehensive property information in a single extract.

What does a technical passport show?

It shows the building's construction details, floor plans, room dimensions, and total area, prepared by a licensed technical inventory organisation.

Who can request a cadastral extract in Belarus?

The registered owner, their authorised representative, or a notary acting on behalf of the parties can request a cadastral extract from the territorial state registration office.
