Family Law

Legal rules governing marriage, divorce, parental rights, custody, child support, and other family relationships.

Definition

Family law in Belarus governs the legal relationships within families including marriage, divorce, parental rights, custody, child support, inheritance within families, and protection from domestic abuse. The Family Code establishes the rights and obligations of family members. Family law addresses both the legal recognition of family relationships and the protections and obligations that arise from those relationships.

Marriage in Belarus is a legal contract between two persons creating mutual rights and obligations. Marriage requires parties to be of legal age, to consent freely to the marriage, and to comply with formal requirements including registration with civil authorities. Marriage creates property rights, inheritance rights, and mutual obligations to support. The law presumes marital property is jointly owned unless shown to be separate property acquired before marriage or by gift or inheritance.

Divorce in Belarus is available when parties consent to the divorce or when one party can demonstrate grounds such as irreconcilable differences. The divorce process addresses property division, custody of children, child support, and spousal support if appropriate. Parties can negotiate a divorce agreement addressing these matters, which is more efficient than disputed litigation. If parties cannot agree, courts determine issues of property, custody, and support based on fairness and the best interests of any children.

Parental rights and obligations include the right to custody and the obligation to provide financial support, healthcare, and education. Both mothers and fathers have equal parental rights. Custody can be joint or sole, depending on the arrangement best serving the child's interests. Child support is calculated based on the parent's income and the child's needs. Parents continue to have support obligations even if custody is changed.

Domestic violence in Belarus is illegal and criminal. Victims of domestic abuse have access to protective orders preventing the abuser from contacting or approaching them. Victims can also pursue criminal charges. Resources including counseling and shelters are available to domestic violence victims. Children witnessing domestic violence can be harmed, and abuse affecting children can be grounds for removing custody.

Key Facts

  • Family law governs marriage, divorce, parental rights, custody, child support, and domestic relationships.
  • Marriage creates mutual rights and obligations, and property is presumed jointly owned unless separate.
  • Divorce requires consent or grounds; property, custody, and support must be determined.
  • Both parents have equal rights and obligations regarding custody and child support.
  • Domestic violence is illegal; victims have access to protective orders and support.

Common Mistake

Parents fail to establish legal custody and child support agreements, leading to disputes and lack of enforcement.

Expert Tip

Execute written custody and child support agreements, registered with courts, to establish clear rights and ensure enforcement.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are parental rights determined if parents are not married?

Unmarried parents can establish parental rights through acknowledgment of paternity or court determination. Both parents have equal rights.

What is considered in determining child custody?

Courts consider the best interests of the child, the child's preferences if old enough, each parent's ability to provide care, and the strength of the parent-child relationship.

How is child support calculated?

Child support is calculated based on the obligor parent's income and the child's needs and standard of living.
