Administrative Procedure

Rules and procedures governing how administrative agencies make decisions affecting rights, including notice, hearings, and appeal rights.

Definition

Administrative procedure in Belarus establishes rules for how government agencies must operate when making decisions affecting the rights and interests of citizens and businesses. Procedural rules ensure that decisions are made fairly, based on complete information, and in accordance with law. The Administrative Procedure Code establishes the requirements for agencies regarding notice, opportunity to be heard, written decisions, and appeal rights.

When administrative agencies make decisions affecting individuals or businesses, they must provide notice to the affected party. Notice must clearly explain what decision is being considered and when the affected party can present their views. Affected parties have the right to be heard before a decision is made. They can present evidence, testimony, and arguments in support of their position. Agencies must consider all relevant information presented before making decisions.

Administrative agencies must issue written decisions explaining the legal and factual basis for their determination. Decisions must specify which facts the agency found established, what law applies, and how the law was applied to reach the decision. This requirement allows affected parties to understand the agency's reasoning and provides the basis for appealing if the decision is believed to be incorrect. Decisions must also specify appeal rights and procedures.

Persons dissatisfied with administrative decisions can appeal through several procedures. Most agencies have internal appeal procedures. If internal appeal does not resolve the matter, administrative courts can review agency decisions. Administrative courts examine whether the agency had legal authority for its decision, followed proper procedures, based the decision on evidence, and did not abuse its discretion.

Rights of citizens and businesses regarding administrative decisions include the right to access files and documents the agency relied on, the right to request that the agency explain its decision, and the right to challenge decisions believed to be unlawful. If a person prevails in an administrative court, the original decision can be reversed and the matter remanded to the agency for reconsideration. Costs may be awarded against an agency that acts unlawfully.

Key Facts

  • Administrative procedure requires agencies to provide notice and opportunity to be heard before making decisions.
  • Agencies must issue written decisions explaining legal and factual basis for their determination.
  • Affected parties have appeal rights through internal procedures and administrative courts.
  • Administrative courts review whether agencies acted lawfully and within their authority.
  • Citizens and businesses have rights to access information agencies relied on and to challenge decisions.

Common Mistake

Individuals accept adverse administrative decisions without appealing or pursuing available remedies.

Expert Tip

If an agency decision affects your rights, request written explanation and consider appealing through available procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What must an agency do before making a decision affecting my rights?

Agencies must provide notice and opportunity for you to present evidence, testimony, and arguments.

Can I appeal an administrative decision?

Yes, you can appeal through the agency's internal procedures and, if unresolved, to administrative courts.

What can an administrative court do if an agency acted unlawfully?

Administrative courts can reverse unlawful decisions, order the agency to reconsider, or award other remedies.
