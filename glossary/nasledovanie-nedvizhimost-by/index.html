Nasledovaniye Nedvizhimosti (Спадкаванне нерухомасці)

The inheritance of real estate in Belarus

Nasledovaniye nedvizhimosti refers to the process by which real property passes to heirs upon the death of the registered owner in Belarus. Inheritance is governed by Section V of the Civil Code of the Republic of Belarus and proceeds either by will (po zaveshchaniyu) or by operation of law (po zakonu).

Upon the death of a property owner, a notary opens a hereditary proceeding (nasledstvennoe delo). Heirs have six months from the date of death to declare their acceptance of the inheritance. Acceptance can be express (filing a statement with the notary) or tacit (taking actual possession of property).

How It Works in Practice

After the six-month period and resolution of all claims, the notary issues a certificate of inheritance (svidetelstvo o prave na nasledstvo) to each heir. This certificate is the document used to register the transfer of ownership in the unified state register.

Heirs who are legal successors by will receive only what the will specifies, subject to the mandatory share (obyazatelnaya dolya) rules protecting minor children, disabled spouses, and certain other vulnerable dependants, who receive at least half the share they would have received under intestate succession regardless of the will's contents.

Key Facts

Governing LawCivil Code of the Republic of Belarus, Section V
Acceptance PeriodSix months from date of death
Key DocumentSvidetelstvo o prave na nasledstvo (inheritance certificate)
Mandatory ShareProtects minor children, disabled spouse — at least half intestate share
Buyer SafetyWait for full registration in unified state register before purchasing from heirs

Buyers purchasing from heirs should verify that the inheritance certificate has been issued, all heirs have accepted or renounced their inheritance, and the new owners have been registered in the unified state register. Purchasing before registration exposes the buyer to challenges from heirs who have not yet formalised their position.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes agree to purchase from heirs who have not yet received their inheritance certificate, relying on a preliminary agreement. If disputes arise among heirs or a mandatory share claim is made, the purchase can be frozen for years.
Expert Tip: If purchasing from an heir, request the notary's confirmation that all heirs have declared acceptance or renunciation and that no mandatory share claims are outstanding. This pre-signing check is far simpler than resolving a dispute after the fact.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do heirs have to claim their inheritance in Belarus?

Heirs must declare acceptance of the inheritance within six months of the owner's death. Late acceptance requires a court order.

What is the mandatory share (obyazatelnaya dolya)?

It is the minimum inheritance share guaranteed to minor children, disabled spouses, and certain other protected persons, regardless of what a will says — at least half of the share they would receive under intestate succession.

When should I buy a property from an heir in Belarus?

Only after the inheritance certificate has been issued by the notary and the heir has been registered as the new owner in the unified state register. Earlier purchase carries significant title risk.
