Ipoteka (Іпатэка)

A mortgage or real estate security right in Belarus

Ipoteka is a real property security right whereby a lender acquires a priority claim over a specific property to secure loan repayment in Belarus. The borrower retains possession and use of the property, but the lender can enforce through judicial sale in the event of default.

A mortgage in Belarus must be established by a notarised mortgage agreement (dogovor ipoteki) and registered in the unified state register. The mortgage ranks by its registration date — earlier-registered mortgages have priority over later ones. Banks operating in Belarus are supervised by the National Bank of the Republic of Belarus.

How It Works in Practice

Belarus maintains state-supported mortgage lending programmes, particularly for housing, administered through state banks such as Belarusbank and ASB Savings Bank. These programmes often offer preferential rates for families with children and for construction of new housing.

Upon full repayment, the lender must cooperate with the borrower to discharge the mortgage from the state register. Buyers purchasing a mortgaged property must ensure the existing mortgage is fully discharged before or simultaneously with the transfer of ownership.

Key Facts

CreationNotarised mortgage agreement + state register entry
State BanksBelarusbank, ASB — state housing mortgage programmes
PriorityBy registration date in unified state register
DischargeLender must cooperate; entry removed from state register
EnforcementJudicial auction via Belarusian courts

Enforcement of a defaulted mortgage proceeds through the Belarusian courts, typically via a judicial auction. Belarus law provides some protections for borrowers in residential mortgage enforcement, including notice periods and some restrictions on enforcement against the sole family home.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes agree to purchase a property without checking whether it carries a registered mortgage. A fresh extract from the unified state register on the day of signing will reveal any registered encumbrances.
Expert Tip: If purchasing with a mortgage, coordinate with the bank to ensure the seller's mortgage discharge and your new mortgage registration happen simultaneously. The notary handling the transaction can coordinate this with both banks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a mortgage need to be notarised in Belarus?

Yes. The mortgage agreement must be notarised and registered in the unified state register to be legally effective against third parties.

Are there state mortgage programmes in Belarus?

Yes. State banks, particularly Belarusbank and ASB, offer preferential mortgage programmes for housing, especially for families with children.

How is a mortgage discharged in Belarus?

After full repayment, the lender and borrower jointly apply to the state registration authority to remove the mortgage entry from the unified state register.
