Sdelka s Nedvizhimostyu (Здзелка з нерухомасцю)

A real estate transaction — the general term for buying, selling or transferring property in Belarus

Sdelka s nedvizhimostyu is the general legal term for any transaction involving real property in Belarus — purchase, sale, gift, exchange, or long-term lease. Each type of transaction has specific formal requirements, but all share the common feature of mandatory state registration.

The most important formal requirements for real estate transactions in Belarus are: (1) written form; (2) notarisation or execution at the state registration authority; and (3) state registration of the resulting right. Failure to comply with any of these requirements renders the transaction void or voidable.

How It Works in Practice

Before entering into any real estate transaction, Belarusian law requires the disclosure of all known encumbrances, restrictions, and third-party rights affecting the property. A seller who conceals material information faces civil liability for damages and, in some cases, criminal liability for fraud.

Currency restrictions apply to real estate transactions in Belarus. Transactions between residents must be settled in Belarusian rubles. Transactions involving non-residents may require National Bank approval or compliance with specific foreign exchange regulations.

Key Facts

FormsPurchase, sale, gift, exchange, long-term lease — all require state registration
Formal RequirementsWritten + notarised + state registered
DisclosureSeller must disclose all encumbrances — concealment = civil/criminal liability
CurrencyResident-to-resident: BYN only; non-resident transactions regulated
Corporate TransactionsAdditional approvals and filings may be required

Legal entities and entrepreneurs engaging in real estate transactions face additional requirements, including compliance with corporate governance rules (board approval for significant transactions), accounting regulations, and, in some cases, anti-monopoly approval for large transactions.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes rely on a preliminary agreement (predvaritelny dogovor) as if it were the final transaction. A preliminary agreement creates an obligation to transact but does not transfer ownership — only the final notarised and registered contract does.
Expert Tip: For any significant real estate transaction, engage a Belarusian lawyer (yurist) specialising in property law to review the documentation before signing. The complexity of Belarusian property law and currency regulations makes independent legal advice essential.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the mandatory requirements for a real estate transaction in Belarus?

The transaction must be in writing, notarised (or executed at the state registration authority), and registered with the State Property Committee. All three steps are required for the transaction to be legally effective.

Can a foreigner buy real estate in Belarus?

Yes, but with restrictions. Foreigners can own residential apartments in multi-storey buildings but face restrictions on acquiring land. Currency exchange and transfer regulations also apply.

What is a predvaritelny dogovor?

It is a preliminary agreement committing both parties to enter into the final transaction. It creates legal obligations but does not transfer ownership — that requires the final notarised and registered contract.
